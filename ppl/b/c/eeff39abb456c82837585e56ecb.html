        The Western New Yorker
        June 19, 1919

        Charles Smith died at the family home on the West Hill on Saturday, June 7, age 75 years and 8 days. He was born in Orangeville on May 30, 1844, son of Philip and Philipena Dick Smith. On March 12, 1868 he was united in marriage to Miss Louisa Herman of Buffalo who died on July 27, 1892. He was again married on April 25, 1894 to Miss Dorothy Hausauer of Buffalo who survives. He was a member of the 136th Regiment and served three years in the Civil War. He is survived by two sons, William C. and Edwin G. Smith of Warsaw;; two grandchildren, Clara Louise and Edna Irene Smith; two brothers, Henry and J.P. Smith of Warsaw; three sisters, Mrs William Miller, Mrs. Sarah Sergel of Warsaw, and Mrs. William Werner of Orangewille, One daughter Clara Louise died in infancy. Served in the Civil War for 3 years.
        Funeral services were held at two o'clock Tuesday afternoon from his late home, Rev. Rothen officiating, assisted by Rev F.W. Schwenk of Buffalo and Ernest Sergel of Warsaw. The G.A.R. of which he was a member, the W.R.C. and the S.O.V. were all represented at the funeral. Interment was in the Warsaw cemetery.
        Those from out of town who were present at the funeral services were Mr. and Mrs. Fred L. Goetz of East Aurora; Bert Webster and family and Mrs. Mary Haselbauer of Corning; Mrs. John Roberts of Batavia; Rev. A.B. Sherman Ellicottville; Mrs. Arthur Walker of Bennington; Mr. and Mrs. Strayline of Wyoming; Fanny Herman, Donald Herman and Rev. F.W. Schwenk of Buffalo.
