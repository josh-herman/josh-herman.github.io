Event Note

When the call came for volunteers from New York State to assist in the great Civil War, he answered the call by enlisting in August 1862, at the age of 19. He served with distinction until June, 1865, taking part in the battles of Gettysburg, Lookout Mountain and Mission Ridge, then joining the Western Army marching through Tennessee and Georgia. At Resaca, Georgia, he was wounded in 1864. In marching from Savannah to Goldsboro in 1865, he was taken prisoner, and placed in Libby Prison at Richmond, Virginia for 19 days. He was then placed in a parole camp for two weeks, at length of which time he received a furlough for thirty days. He received his discharge at Annapolis, Maryland at the close of the War in 1865.

Here was a family coming to the United States in 1841, and only twenty-one years later sending one of its sons to fight in an internal war. Like most of the northern immigrants, Christopher Fisher fought on the side of the Union.

        Obituary
        Christopher Fisher, 76 years old died last night at his farm home in the town of Orangeville. He was born in Greenbush, Herkimer county in May 1843 and had lived on the west hill about 70 years. March 7, 1867, Mr. Fisher was married to Louisa Christ who with 2 daughters and 4 sons survive; Mrs. Henry Meissal and Mrs. Burton Hall of Quakertown, Walter and Glenn Fisher of Warsaw, Charles of Lamont and Frank of Wyoming, also one brother, Andrew Fisher of Varysburg.
        A prayer service will be held at the home on Sunday afternoon at one o'clock and at two o'clock the funeral service will be held in the German Evangelical church, the Rev. J. Rothen officiating and the body will be taken to Wethersfield for burial. Mr. Fisher had lived in this vicinity for about 70 years and was a well known farmer and much esteemed citizen.
