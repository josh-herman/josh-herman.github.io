      • General:

        Mrs. Anna Eve Krauss, 96, a resident of Warsaw for many years passed away at the Eastside Nursing Home on Monday, April 9th. She was born in Buffalo on October 20, 1854, the daughter of Henry and Rosina Blecher Pfeifer and on June 4, 1874 she married Mr. Krauss who passed away March 23, 1919. Surviving her are 2 children, Irving Krauss of Perry, Mrs. Ray Munger of Warsaw, 11 grandchildren and 7 great grandchildren. A daughter, Mrs. Horace Goodale passed away several years ago. She also leaves 2 brothers, Jacob Pfeifer of East Aurora and Rudolph Pfeifer of Warsaw. Rev. Thomas E. Asbury officiated. Mrs. Krauss was the oldest member of the Warsaw United church.

        Died Sunday night, March 23rd, about 7:00. He had been in failing health during the winter but on Sunday was about as usual and death was very sudden at the last. Mr. Krauss was born in Bennington, N.Y., August 25, 1850 and has lived most of his life in the county. He was married June 4, 1874 to Miss Anna Pfeiffer of Orangeville, who survives him. Besides his wife he leaves one son and two daughters, Irving Krauss of Perry, Mrs. Ray Munger and Mrs. Horace Goodale of Warsaw. Also 4 brothers, Andrew, Christ, George and Charles and 4 sisters, Mrs. Dan Herman, Mrs. Charles Schafer, Mrs. Henry Rice and Mrs. John Korn all of Buffalo, NY. Funeral services were held Wednesday afternoon at 2:00 at his late home on Clinton avenue. Rev. Morgan Millar, officiating. Burial in Warsaw cemetery.
