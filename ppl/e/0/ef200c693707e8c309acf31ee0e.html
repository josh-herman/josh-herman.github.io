        Wyoming County Times, September 17, 1953
        Jacob Pfeifer, one of East Aurora's oldest inhabitants died Wednesday, September 9th in Meyer Memorial Hospital, Buffalo at the age of 97, He had been in ill health for the past year. Mr. Pfeifer retired many years ago and had made his home for the past seventeen with his son, Howard Pfeifer in East Aurora. Born in Griffinshire, now known as Griffin's Mills April 27, 1856. Mr. Pfeifer moved with his family to Ebenezer. When he was 10 they moved to Wyoming County. After his marriage, he and his late wife, Elizabeth Bender Pfeifer owned and lived on the same farm near Hermitage for fifty years. Funeral services for Mr. Pfeifer were held in the Howe Funeral Home, East Aurora and Sunday afternoon from the First Baptist church in Hermitage. The burial was in Wethersfield Springs. The Rev. Robert H. Emmons of the First Baptist church of East Aurora and the Rev. Thomas E. Grove of the Hermitage Baptist church officiated. Surviving him are 3 sons, Charles H. Pfeifer of Batavia, Howard J. Pfeifer of East Aurora and Clifford A. Pfeifer of Hermitage, 9 grandchildren and 12 great-grandchildren and 6 great great grandchildren. Mr. Pfeifer never forgot the death of President Lincoln, April 15, 1865. He was almost nine years old. "Dad was hauling wood to the Aurora-Buffalo plank road in Ebenezer and from there teams from Buffalo took it into the city," he recalled. "Mike, one of the teamsters, early that morning told us of the assassination. The little village went into mourning because Abe Lincoln was loved by all".
