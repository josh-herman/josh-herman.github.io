      • General:

        Wyoming County Times, Thursday, December 10, 1936:
        Sarah M. Sergel, widow of Charles Sergel died at the home of her son, Henry Sergel of Warsaw on Tuesday, December 8th. She was born in Orangeville on December 2, 1863 and was the daughter of Philip and Filippina Dick Smith. Surviving her are 3 sons, William Sergel of Newark, Ernest Sergel of Ransomville and Henry Sergel of Warsaw, ten grandchildren and 2 great grandchildren, one brother, John P. Smith of Warsaw and 2 sisters, Mrs. William Miller of Warsaw and Mrs. Kate Schneckenburger of Buffalo. The funeral will be held at the Methodist church in Warsaw on Friday, December 11, at 2:30 with the Rev. Oliver Gesell, Rev. J.W. Sanderson and Rev. J.J. Baker officiating. The burial will be in the Warsaw cemetery. Mrs. Sergel was a member of the Evangelical church of Warsaw.

      • General:

        A farmer who had lived in the town of Warsaw for 88 years died at his home. Born in Orangeville, the son of Philip and Phillipine Dick Smith; he was probably the oldest person in Warsaw. In December, 1888 he married Elizabeth Wagenblass who survives him as well as 6 children, George A. Smith, Benjamin D. Smith, Carl P. Smith, Wesley H. Smith, all of Warsaw, Mrs. Arthur B. Welker and Mrs. Dorothy Nevinger of Attica, 19 grandchildren and 16 great grandchildren. Member of the Evangelical United Brethren Church and a charter member of the Dairymen"s League.
